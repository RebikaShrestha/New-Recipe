Momos

Description:

Enjoy the taste of Nepal with our Momos. These dumplings are filled with a delicious mixture of minced meat and spices, then steamed to perfection. Served with a homemade dipping sauce.

Ingredients:

  • Ground meat
  • Flour
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Cilantro
  • Green onions

Steps:

  1. Prepare the filling by mixing ground meat, ginger, garlic, soy sauce, and green onions.
  2. Roll out the dough and fill it with the meat mixture.
  3. Steam the momos until cooked through.
  4. Serve with a dipping sauce made from sesame oil, soy sauce, and cilantro.

Dal Bhat

Description:

Dal Bhat is a traditional Nepali meal consisting of steamed rice (bhat) and lentil soup (dal). It's often accompanied by various side dishes.

Ingredients:

  • Rice
  • Lentils
  • Spices
  • Vegetables
  • Ghee

Steps:

  1. Cook rice and lentils separately.
  2. Prepare a tempering with spices and ghee, then mix it with the cooked lentils.
  3. Serve the dal and rice with various side dishes like vegetables, pickles, and meat if desired.

Sel Roti

Description:

Sel Roti is a traditional Nepali rice-based doughnut. It's crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, often enjoyed with tea.

Ingredients:

  • Rice flour
  • Sugar
  • Milk
  • Cardamom

Steps:

  1. Prepare a batter by mixing rice flour, sugar, milk, and cardamom.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and shape the batter into rings.
  3. Deep-fry until golden brown.
  4. Serve with tea or yogurt.

alutama

Description:

Alu Tama is a popular Nepali dish made with bamboo shoots (tama) and potatoes (alu) in a flavorful curry.

Ingredients:

  • Bamboo shoots
  • Potatoes
  • Spices
  • Tomatoes

Steps:

  1. Cook bamboo shoots and potatoes until tender.
  2. Prepare a curry with spices and tomatoes.
  3. Mix the cooked bamboo shoots and potatoes with the curry.
  4. Serve with rice.

Dhido

Description:

Dhido is a traditional Nepali dish made from buckwheat or millet flour. It has a dense, dough-like consistency and is typically eaten with pickles or vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • Buckwheat or millet flour
  • Water

Steps:

  1. Mix flour with water to form a thick dough.
  2. Shape the dough into small portions.
  3. Serve with pickles or vegetables.

Gundruk

Description:

Gundruk is a fermented leafy green vegetable, often used in Nepali cuisine as a side dish or in soups.

Ingredients:

  • Leafy greens (usually mustard greens)
  • Salt
  • Spices

Steps:

  1. Wash and cut the leafy greens.
  2. Ferment them by adding salt and spices.
  3. Use as a side dish or in soups.

Yomari

Description:

Yomari is a sweet dumpling filled with a mixture of jaggery and sesame seeds, often made during the Yomari Punhi festival in Nepal.

Ingredients:

  • Rice flour
  • Jaggery
  • Sesame seeds

Steps:

  1. Prepare a dough from rice flour.
  2. Fill the dough with a mixture of jaggery and sesame seeds.
  3. Steam until cooked.
  4. Enjoy this sweet treat.

Thukpa

Description:

Thukpa is a hearty Tibetan-Nepali noodle soup made with vegetables, meat, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • Noodles
  • Vegetables
  • Meat (optional)
  • Spices

Steps:

  1. Cook noodles and set aside.
  2. Prepare a broth with vegetables and meat (if desired).
  3. Pour the broth over the noodles.
  4. Serve hot.

Newari Bara

Description:

Newari Bara is a traditional Newari lentil pancake, often served with spicy sauces.

Ingredients:

  • Lentils
  • Spices
  • Oil

Steps:

  1. Soak lentils, grind them into a batter, and add spices.
  2. Heat oil and pour a ladle of batter to make pancakes.
  3. Fry until golden brown.
  4. Serve with spicy sauces.

Chatamari

Description:

Chatamari is often referred to as the 'Nepali Pizza.' It's a thin rice crepe topped with various ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • Rice flour
  • Minced meat or vegetables
  • Spices

Steps:

  1. Make a thin rice crepe.
  2. Top it with minced meat or vegetables and spices.
  3. Cook until the crepe is crispy.
  4. Enjoy your Nepali 'pizza.'
